# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two primary visitors:

1. **Business owners and workplace managers** — employers in high-risk industries (construction, childcare, hospitality, aged care) who need to get their team certified and audit-ready. Compliance is the trigger; they're comparing providers and need fast trust signals: accreditation, local delivery, cert turnaround.

2. **Individuals** — people booking for themselves (community members, parents, sports clubs, coaches). Lower urgency than employers; trust and course clarity are the conversion levers.

Both arrive from local search and want to know: is this real, is this local, can I book easily?

## Product Purpose

Life First Aid Courses delivers nationally recognised first aid training on-site and via blended delivery, based in Blaxland and serving the Blue Mountains and Greater Western Sydney region. The site's job is to convert visitors to bookings — phone call or quote form — by projecting local credibility, clear course offerings, and frictionless next steps.

Success: visitor lands, trusts the provider within seconds, finds their course or use-case, and reaches out.

## Brand Personality

Calm, capable, local.

The experienced person in the room who doesn't need to shout. Confidence that comes from 10+ years of showing up and doing the work. Warm enough to put a nervous first-timer at ease; rigorous enough that a WHS coordinator signs off without hesitation.

Not: alarm, urgency tactics, clinical cold, or impersonal corporate distance.

## Anti-references

- Medical/clinical cold — no hospital-blue sterile layout, no white-coat aesthetic.
- Faceless national training franchise — no "your compliance solution" corporate-speak or generic LMS aesthetics.
- Cheap local-business aesthetic — no clip-art first aid crosses, low-effort badge grids, or budget-template layouts.
- SaaS/startup defaults — no gradient hero cards, cream-on-cream restraint, or AI scaffold patterns.

## Design Principles

1. **Local authority shows, not claims.** Specificity earns trust: place names, real industry names, audit-ready language that speaks to a WHS coordinator's actual concern. No generic "trusted nationwide."
2. **Calm confidence, not alarm.** Safety is high-stakes. The design should feel like a steady expert, not an emergency alert. Urgency comes from context (real injury stats), not from red-on-red button pressure.
3. **Course clarity is a conversion lever.** The course catalogue is the product. It must be easy to scan, understand, and act on — not buried in cards.
4. **Every section earns a next step.** Visitors shouldn't have to hunt for the call or quote action. Friction is the enemy.
5. **The local region is a differentiator.** Blue Mountains and Greater Western Sydney is not a footnote. On-site delivery at your venue, in your area, is the core value prop over online-only competitors.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations. Touch targets ≥44×44px (already enforced via `.btn` min-height). Focus-visible rings on all interactive elements.
